\section*{Tails}

\textbf{Definition}

The tail of a random variable is:

\textbf{Why do we care about tails?}
\begin{itemize}
\item Jobs delayed more than 24 hours
\item Packets dropped due to full buffer
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Key idea}

Mean $\rightarrow$ average behavior (easy)\\
Tail $\rightarrow$ rare extreme events (hard)

\textbf{Why are tails hard to compute?}

Requires summing many small probabilities

\section*{Tails Example-2}

\textbf{Q:}

Jobs arrive to a datacenter according to a Poisson process with rate jobs/hour.\\
What’s the probability that jobs arrive during the first hour?

\textbf{Intuition}
\begin{itemize}
\item Jobs arrive randomly over time
\item Most hours have moderate arrivals
\item Sometimes, we see a burst of arrivals
\item This is a tail event (sudden spike in load)
\end{itemize}

\section*{Why Tail Bounds?}

Exact tail probabilities are often hard to compute

\textbf{Exact computation}

\[
P\{X \ge k\} = \sum_{i=k}^{n} \binom{n}{i} p^i (1-p)^{n-i}
\]

\[
X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda)
\]

\[
P\{X \ge k\} = \sum_{i=k}^{\infty} e^{-\lambda} \frac{\lambda^i}{i!}
\]

Complicated, long sums!

\textbf{Idea:} Instead of exact value $\ldots$

\begin{itemize}
\item We find an upper bound
\item Easier to compute
\item Still gives a guarantee
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Key idea}

Approximate safely instead of computing exactly

\section*{Markov’s Inequality}

\textbf{Key Idea}

Uses only the mean\\
Large values $\Rightarrow$ rare

\textbf{Example}

Avg = 10 $\Rightarrow$ few values can be 100+

\textbf{Markov Inequality}

\[
P(X \ge a) \le \frac{E[X]}{a}
\]

\textbf{Proof (Idea)}

\[
E[X] = \sum_{x=0}^{\infty} x \cdot p_X(x)
\]
\[
\ge \sum_{x=a}^{\infty} x \cdot p_X(x)
\]
\[
\ge \sum_{x=a}^{\infty} a \cdot p_X(x)
\]
\[
= a \cdot P(X \ge a)
\]

\section*{Markov Inequality: Real-Life Example}

Average income = 10,000

\[
P(X \ge 50000) \le \frac{10000}{50000} = 0.2
\]

At most 20\% people can earn 50,000

\section*{Markov Inequality: Solved Example 1}

Average = 70\%, cutoff = 90

\[
P(X \ge 90) \le \frac{70}{90} = \frac{7}{9}
\]

\[
P(X \ge 90) \le 0.778
\]

\section*{Markov Inequality: Solved Example 2}

Average = 5000, threshold = 20000

\[
P(X \ge 20000) \le \frac{5000}{20000} = \frac{1}{4}
\]

\section*{Chebyshev’s Inequality}

\textbf{Key Idea}

Uses mean + variance\\
Controls deviation from mean

\[
P(|X - \mu| \ge a) \le \frac{\text{Var}(X)}{a^2}
\]

\textbf{Proof Idea}

Apply Markov on $(X - \mu)^2$

\section*{Chebyshev on Running Example}

\[
X \sim \text{Binomial}(n, \tfrac{1}{2})
\]
\[
\mu = \frac{n}{2}, \quad \text{Var}(X) = \frac{n}{4}
\]

\section*{Chebyshev Example}

\[
P(|X - 70| \ge 30) \le \frac{10^2}{30^2} = \frac{1}{9}
\]
